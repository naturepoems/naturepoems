When the stars are shining clear,
And the moon is broad and white,
In the woods, I seem to hear,
Nature’s voice, in pure delight.

Every leaf, and every breeze,
Brings a tale of love and bloom,
Language soft, like rustling trees,
Whispers through the twilight gloom.


- William Hazlitt
