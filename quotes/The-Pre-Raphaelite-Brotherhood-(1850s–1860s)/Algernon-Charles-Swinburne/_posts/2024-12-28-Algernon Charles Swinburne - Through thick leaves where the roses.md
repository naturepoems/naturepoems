Through thick leaves where the roses blow,
Where blooms the grass in emerald gold,
There comes a whisper from the boughs,
As if the summer could unfold,
Its secrets to the wandering bee,
And call the sunset to its feet.
- From "The Forest"

- Algernon Charles Swinburne
