The autumn leaves like fire,
Upon the ground do lie,
Each a fading memory,
With a bittersweet goodbye.

Yet in their slow descending,
There’s a beauty in decay,
A lesson from the season,
Of life’s own gentle way.

- Catherine J W Munro
