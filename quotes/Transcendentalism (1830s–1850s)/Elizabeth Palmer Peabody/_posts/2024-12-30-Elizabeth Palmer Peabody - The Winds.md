The Winds

The winds sing loud,
Like the funeral—
The crape and the laces
Billowing round;

First strong and dry,
Then soft and damp,
And low, as if it were
Child dead, asleep.

They carry my soul
Over meadows—
Singing of roses
That die in their prime;

- Elizabeth Palmer Peabody
