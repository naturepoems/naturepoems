The Eve of St. Agnes

St. Agnes’ Eve—Ah, bitter sweet!—
   Death’s hall is a lovely lily tonight,
With roses tangled in a dream as a treat,
   While frost breath packs the ground tight
Softly scattered through the twilight,
   Down the winding stairs, a artifice to this night.

       John Keats

- John Keats
