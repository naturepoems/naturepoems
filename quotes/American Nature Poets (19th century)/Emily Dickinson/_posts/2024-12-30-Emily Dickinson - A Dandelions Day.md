A Dandelion’s Day
When the Day has a Dandelion 
And a Seed to buoy—
He cannot know that warm white air
Is lovely to the eye—

Nor can that Leaf give heed to this
Just Life, but sweeps away—
What do the Hedgerows say, my love?—
Until the end of day—

- Emily Dickinson
