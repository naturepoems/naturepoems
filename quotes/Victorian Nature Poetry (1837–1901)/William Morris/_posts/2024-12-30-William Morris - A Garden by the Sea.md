And trees, no less than men,
Are made of dust, and to the dust,
They shall return, the owners say,
Yet blossoms bloom, and robins sing.

The winds are wild and far,
Yet calm, in wood and mire:
In all the majesty of blue,
With stars that bloom beyond desire.

- William Morris
