The brook flows like a silver vein,
Through valleys cloaked in emerald wed,
Whispering tales of joy and pain,
Along the fields where angels tread.

May nature’s whispers find our heart,
Let us from her vast treasures glean,
And with each note play a part,
In the harmony of the serene.

– George William Curtis
