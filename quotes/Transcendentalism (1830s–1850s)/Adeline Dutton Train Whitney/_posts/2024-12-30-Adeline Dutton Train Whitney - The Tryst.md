The Tryst
Where the river softly glides,
And the woodland thrush abides,
In the evening's golden hue,
I will wait for you, my true.
In the blend of dusk and day,
When the sky is pink and gray,
By the moss-covered stone, I’ll stand,
With the wild blossoms in my hand.

— Adeline Dutton Train Whitney
