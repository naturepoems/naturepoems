A Winter's Day
The sun is gone—its light, its heat,
The winter's air is chill and sweet,
The skies are gray; the earth is white,
But o'er the hedges dim and bright,
The colonies of boughs are brave,
And hid in dreams of hope I crave.






- Percy Bysshe Shelley
