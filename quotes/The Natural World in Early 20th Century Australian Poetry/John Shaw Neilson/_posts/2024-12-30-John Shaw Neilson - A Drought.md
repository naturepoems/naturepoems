A Drought

Where golden fields lie withered, bare,
    And drought is in the sigh;
I watch the weary winds despair,
    As souls begin to die.

Yet in the glimmer of the night,
    A whisper comes to me:
For every wrong must turn to right,
    And every heart is free.

- John Shaw Neilson
