A land of gold, of emerald roots,
  Where skies with mourning look—
  I gather dusky untold fruits,
  By shadow'd river brook.

Upon the leafy lanes I sit,
  Inspired by scents often bred;
  Dreaming a thought where past do fit,
  As night slips into bed.

- William Hazlitt
