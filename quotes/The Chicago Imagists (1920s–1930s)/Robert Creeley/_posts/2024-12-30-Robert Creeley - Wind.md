Wind

Whistling through
  and bending,
a soft breath,
a cool pulse—
yet carries the heat
in limbs of the earth;
together we are rooted,
each call away
  from warmth,
from the push,
  pulls the clouds,
inviting the rain;
it brings us
  what we might hold.


- Robert Creeley
