Sphinx

   The seas cling beneath
      stillness,
     to earth’s tremor,
  they lift me,” she spoke,
  leapt through darkness,
but you, across her distance
  in the silence, nothing above—
     are not shape but shadow,
desire, feathered
   like hummingbirds.
     —H.D.
