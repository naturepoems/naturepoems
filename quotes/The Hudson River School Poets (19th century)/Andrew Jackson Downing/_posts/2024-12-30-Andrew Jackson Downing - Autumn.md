Autumn
Oh, the autumn's golden glow,
As leaves like flames descend,
Each a memory of summer's warmth,
Each a friend in the end.
The fields wear a russet hue,
While crisp winds carry the scent,
Of the harvest, ripe and bounteous,
Nature's sweet testament.

- Andrew Jackson Downing
