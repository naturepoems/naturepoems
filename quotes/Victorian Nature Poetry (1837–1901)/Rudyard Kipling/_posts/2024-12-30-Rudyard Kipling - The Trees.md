The trees have ears and the stones may tell,  
If yonder skies could relent;  
For every hour they keep a sound,  
Of Nature's sweet lament.  
 
The river runs between the roots,  
In whispered tones it sings;  
A symphony composed in time,  
Of all the earth’s offerings.

- Rudyard Kipling
