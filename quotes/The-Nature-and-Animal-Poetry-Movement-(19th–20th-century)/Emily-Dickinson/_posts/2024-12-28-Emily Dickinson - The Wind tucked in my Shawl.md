The Wind tucked in my Shawl —
The Sweet — unwhipped of Wind —
The air is hot, the day is warm,
And I must stretch my hand,

To clasp its yellow plays —
The sun is, too, by eye —
For what is all so rare and fine
And Temperance — when we can dream? —

- Emily Dickinson
