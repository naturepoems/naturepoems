Mountains and Rivers Without End
Mountains and rivers without end,
Dawn comes softly in the east;
the sun rises behind the valley,
drenched in mist and cloud.

Plum blossoms fall without naming,
wind plays through the pines.
In a poem of changes,
a horizon sighs above the ridge.

— Gary Snyder
