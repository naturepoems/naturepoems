Softly the leaves are falling,
A whisper in the autumn air,
Their golden deaths are calling,
To linger and lay bare.
Amid the fading flowers,
Nature draws her final breath,
As the sun departs for hours,
And the world prepares for death.

- Dorothy Wordsworth
