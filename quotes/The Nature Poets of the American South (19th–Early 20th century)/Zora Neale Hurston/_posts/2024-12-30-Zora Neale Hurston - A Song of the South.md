Oh, to sing a song of the South,
Where the earth is rich with fruitful mouth,
Where magnolias scent the air,
And the sun lingers with tender care.

The rivers run wild and free,
Whispering tales of time to me,
In every gust, a softer tone,
In every shade, a love I’ve known.
