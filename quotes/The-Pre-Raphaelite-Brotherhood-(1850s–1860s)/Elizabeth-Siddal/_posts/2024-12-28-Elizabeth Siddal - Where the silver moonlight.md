Where the silver moonlight darkens,
And the leaves are palely spread,
In the shade of a weary willow
Lies the love-point of the dead.

A soft sigh of the sunset,
A whisper, slow, and sweet,
And the balmy spirit walks,
That the dews and night winds meet.


- Elizabeth Siddal
