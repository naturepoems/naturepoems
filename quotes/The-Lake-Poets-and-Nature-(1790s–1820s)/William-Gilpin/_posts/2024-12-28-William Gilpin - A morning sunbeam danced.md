A morning sunbeam danced upon
The leaf-strown glade, where once were grown
The team of oaken-trees; now none
Are left, save grass and shadow.
Yet still the song of birds resounds,
In sweet arpeggio resounds,
And echoes through the verdant grounds
As Nature's own bravado.

- William Gilpin
