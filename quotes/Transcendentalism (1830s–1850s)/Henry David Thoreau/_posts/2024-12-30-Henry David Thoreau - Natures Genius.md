Nature is full of genius,
But only man is a great thinker.
The wildest creatures boast more thrift,
Than our own clumsy laborer.

In the dark woods, I seek my muse,
The rustling leaves sing sweetly,
Whispered secrets from the trees,
That keep my heart from being weary.

- Henry David Thoreau
