When winter's chill enfolds the land,
And snowflakes softly fall,
A quiet stillness takes command,
And nature's hush enthralls.

Yet 'neath the frost, the roots do sleep,
In dreams of springtime bright,
And soon the world, from slumber deep,
Will wake to joy and light.

- Ethel Turner
