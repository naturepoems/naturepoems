A shrub of myrtle, faint and far,
  Where workers bend their heads,
  Whence gentler breezes blow this way,
  To a heart by mem'ries led.

O tree whose leafy bower I see!
  The stone that blocks the vale,
  Where once I lingered close to thee
  And shaded by the snail.

- William Hazlitt
