The Snow Man

One must have a mind of winter
To regard the frost and the boughs
Of the pine-trees crusted with snow;
And have been cold a long time
To behold the juniper 
Tangled in the snow,
With the spruces rough in the distant glitter
Of the January sun;
And not to think
Of any misery in the sound
Of the wind, that is not misfortunes
