In solitude, the oak stands,
Crowned in green and gold,
Glorying amidst the vines,
Breathing deep the solemn earth.
Its roots entwine,
With history rich,
And storied boughs,
With sheltering love,
For the wanderer and the lost,
In the great wild heart of peace.

— **Virginia Woolf, The Oak**
