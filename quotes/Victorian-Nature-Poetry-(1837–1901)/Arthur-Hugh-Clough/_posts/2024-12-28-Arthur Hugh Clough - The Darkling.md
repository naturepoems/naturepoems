The Darkling Thrush

I leant upon a coppice gate
   When Frost was spectre-gray,
And Winter's dregs made desolate
   The weakening eye of day.

The tangled herbaceous undergrowth
   Hanging on the darkling thorns,
Beneath a canopy of faith,
   Starved with the wear of fawns.


- Arthur Hugh Clough
