The palm

So high upon a beetle,
grown by seas of crowds, a rising sand,
as I rest lifted, a green lightly treads.
The earth is brittle,
quaint in texture, and barges all about;
the breezes rub "upon these," circles round
once both shrunk and extended—  
how can we keep this weight of the high?

- HD
